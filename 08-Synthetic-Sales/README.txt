# Sales Data Visualization with Functions

## Overview
This project was created to practice building **visuals in Python using functions**.  
Instead of writing plotting code inline, I wrapped each visualization in reusable functions to ensure consistency, scalability, and professionalism.

## Data
- The dataset is **randomly generated synthetic sales data**.
- Columns include: Date, Product, Region, Units Sold, Unit Price, and Revenue.

## Visuals
Six key visuals were created:
1. Monthly Revenue Trend (line chart)
2. Revenue by Product (bar chart)
3. Revenue by Region (bar chart)
4. Product Share of Total Revenue (pie chart)
5. Product-Region Heatmap (seaborn heatmap)
6. Units Sold vs Revenue (scatter plot)

## Insights
- Product D dominates revenue due to high unit price.
- Product C sells many units but contributes less revenue.
- Regional performance is uneven, with South and East leading.
- Seasonal peaks in March and April suggest demand cycles.

## Purpose
The goal was to:
- Practice **function-based visualization design**.
- Create a **presentation-ready theme** for charts.
- Generate a **hard copy report** with insights and recommendations.
